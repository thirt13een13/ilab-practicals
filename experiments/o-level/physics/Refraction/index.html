Procedure

  1. Place the glass block on the drawing board and trace its outline onto the paper.
  2. Set the normal location d from the edge of the glass block before tracing the ray.
  3. Mark a point on the incidence face and use the set square to draw the normal, perpendicular to the surface, at that point.
  4. Position the ray box so the incident ray strikes the marked point at your chosen angle of incidence, i.
  5. Trace the ray's path as it bends towards the normal while entering the denser glass, travels through the block, then bends away from the normal as it emerges back into air.
  6. Use the protractor to measure the angle of refraction, r, from the normal.
  7. Repeat for a range of angles of incidence and record each i–r pair.
  8. Plot sin(i) against sin(r). The gradient of the best-fit line is the refractive index, n.
  9. Switch to the triangular prism to explore dispersion and prism refraction.
n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂  |  n = sin i / sin r  |  sin θc = 1/n
Lateral displacement: d = t·sin(i − r) / cos r

Air is taken as n = 1.00 throughout this simulation.
